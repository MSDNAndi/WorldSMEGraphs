# Diabetic Foot Bypass V2 - Story Idea

## Core Concept

A comprehensive medical education story about critical limb-threatening ischemia in a diabetic patient, featuring Dr. Young Erben's microsurgical expertise and emphasis on prevention, early intervention, and patient empowerment.

## Character: Dr. Young Erben

- **Ethnicity**: Korean
- **Physical**: Petite, 40s, expressive rectangular black-framed glasses
- **Languages**: Spanish (Venezuelan upbringing), English, German, Korean
- **Specialty**: Vascular Surgery with microsurgical expertise
- **Character Arc**: Demonstrates patience, precision, and preventive education

## Patient: Maria Torres

- **Age**: 62
- **Background**: Venezuelan immigrant, worked as restaurant cook for 40 years
- **Diabetes**: Type 2, 15 years, poorly controlled (HbA1c 9.2%)
- **Presentation**: Non-healing ulcer on right foot, pain at rest, can't work
- **Socioeconomic**: No insurance initially, delayed care due to cost
- **Cultural**: Spanish-speaking, family-oriented, worried about burden on children

## Story Hook

Maria presents with a non-healing foot ulcer that threatens her livelihood and independence. Dr. Erben must not only perform technically challenging distal bypass surgery but also address the systemic issues (healthcare access, diabetes management) that led to this crisis. The story emphasizes that this was preventable with earlier intervention.

## Panel Count: 35 Panels

**Act 1**: Recognition & Diagnosis (10 panels)  
**Act 2**: Surgical Planning (5 panels)  
**Act 3**: Microsurgical Bypass (10 panels)  
**Act 4**: Recovery & Prevention (10 panels)
