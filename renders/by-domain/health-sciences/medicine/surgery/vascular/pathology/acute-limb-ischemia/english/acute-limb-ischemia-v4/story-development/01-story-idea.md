# Acute Limb Ischemia V4 - Story Idea

## Title: "Diego's First Emergency"

## Main Characters

### Dr. Young Erben
- **Role**: Vascular surgery researcher and educator, mentor figure
- **Age**: 35
- **Appearance**: Professional lab coat, confident warm smile, dark hair in practical style
- **Personality**: Patient teacher, encouraging, knowledgeable

### Diego
- **Role**: Research intern (Mexican heritage)
- **Age**: 21
- **Appearance**: Glasses, neat dark hair, eager expression, scrubs
- **Personality**: Curious, detail-oriented, a bit nervous but determined

### Supporting Characters
- Camila and Camilo (fellow interns, brief appearances)
- Mr. Rodriguez (patient, 62-year-old with sudden leg pain)

## Story Premise

Diego is on his first overnight call with Dr. Young Erben when a patient arrives with sudden severe leg pain. Through the emergency, Diego learns to recognize the "6 Ps" of acute arterial occlusion (Pain, Pallor, Pulselessness, Paresthesia, Paralysis, Poikilothermia) and understands why time is critical in limb ischemia.

## Educational Goals

1. **Primary**: Recognize the 6 Ps of acute limb ischemia
2. **Secondary**: Understand the time-critical nature ("Time is muscle")
3. **Tertiary**: Learn the diagnostic workup and treatment options

## Emotional Arc

- **Opening**: Diego nervous about first overnight call
- **Rising Action**: Patient arrives, Diego feels overwhelmed
- **Turning Point**: Dr. Erben guides Diego through systematic assessment
- **Resolution**: Diego recognizes the condition, patient goes to surgery
- **Closing**: Diego gains confidence, Dr. Erben praises his observation skills

## Visual Style

Dora-the-Explorer cartoon style with:
- Thick black outlines
- Flat bright colors (hospital greens, blues, warm skin tones)
- Expressive cartoon faces
- Educational labels and callouts

## Target Audience

- Medical students
- Nursing students
- Healthcare professionals in training
- Patients seeking to understand the condition

## Unique Elements (Different from other stories)

- Focus on overnight emergency setting
- Mentor-student teaching dynamic
- Diego's personal growth journey
- Introduction to the "6 Ps" mnemonic
