# Acute Limb Ischemia: A Race Against Time

## Story Overview

An emergency vascular surgery story featuring **Dr. Young Erben**, a Korean-American vascular surgeon, who must save a Venezuelan immigrant's leg in a dramatic race against the clock. The story emphasizes the golden 6-hour window for limb salvage, the importance of atrial fibrillation management, and the power of multilingual, culturally-sensitive care.

### Character: Dr. Young Erben

- **Ethnicity**: Korean
- **Physical**: Petite, 40s, distinctive expressive rectangular glasses
- **Languages**: Spanish (Venezuelan upbringing), English, German, Korean
- **Specialty**: Vascular Surgery
- **Approach**: Culturally competent, multilingual, compassionate yet technically excellent

### Patient: Carlos Ramirez

- **Age**: 68
- **Background**: Venezuelan immigrant
- **Condition**: Acute left leg ischemia from atrial fibrillation embolus
- **Presentation**: "Dead leg" - pale, pulseless, paralyzed
- **Cultural Connection**: Spanish language allows trust and communication

## Story Structure (35 Panels)

### Act 1: Emergency Recognition (Panels 1-10)
The 3 AM emergency call, dramatic ER presentation, systematic 6 Ps assessment, and building trust through Spanish language connection.

**Key Medical Content**:
- 6 Ps of acute limb ischemia
- Time-critical decision making
- Finding atrial fibrillation as cause
- Informed consent process

### Act 2: Surgical Preparation (Panels 11-18)
Team mobilization, immediate anticoagulation, patient reassurance, and OR preparation.

**Key Medical Content**:
- Emergency team coordination
- Heparin administration
- Surgical planning
- Spinal anesthesia

### Act 3: Surgical Procedure (Panels 19-28)
The Fogarty catheter thromboembolectomy procedure in detail.

**Key Medical Content**:
- Femoral artery exposure
- Arteriotomy technique
- Fogarty balloon catheter use
- Clot extraction
- Patch angioplasty closure

### Act 4: Recovery and Prevention (Panels 29-35)
Immediate recovery, explanation of cause, and long-term prevention planning.

**Key Medical Content**:
- Immediate limb reperfusion
- Atrial fibrillation as embolic source
- Stroke risk education
- Long-term anticoagulation
- System improvement

## Educational Objectives

### Medical Learning Goals

1. **Recognition**: Identify acute limb ischemia using 6 Ps assessment
2. **Timing**: Understand the critical 6-hour golden window
3. **Etiology**: Differentiate embolic vs thrombotic causes
4. **Treatment**: Learn Fogarty catheter thromboembolectomy technique
5. **Prevention**: Understand importance of anticoagulation in AFib
6. **Systems**: Recognize and address system failures

### Cultural Learning Goals

1. **Language**: See the power of multilingual care
2. **Trust**: Building rapport across cultural differences
3. **Family**: Involving family in medical decisions
4. **Empowerment**: Patient education and system advocacy
5. **Accountability**: Acknowledging healthcare system failures
