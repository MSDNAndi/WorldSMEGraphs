## Relative Size Ratios

To understand the extreme scales:

| Comparison | Ratio |
|------------|-------|
| **Planck length : Proton** | 1 : 10^20 |
| **Proton : Human** | 1 : 10^15 |
| **Human : Solar System** | 1 : 10^13 |
| **Solar System : Galaxy** | 1 : 10^8 |
| **Planck length : Universe** | 1 : 10^61 |

**Mind-Blowing Fact**: If you scaled up the Planck length to the size of a grain of sand (1mm), then:
- A proton would be 100,000 km across (larger than Earth!)
- An atom would be the size of the solar system
- A human would be 100 light-years tall
- The observable universe would span 10^53 meters (vastly larger than actual universe)

## Physical Phenomena at Different Scales

### Macroscopic (10^0 m)
- Classical mechanics works perfectly
- Quantum effects negligible
- Gravity weak but measurable

### Atomic (10^-10 m)
- Quantum mechanics essential
- Electrons in probability clouds
- Gravity still negligible

### Nuclear (10^-15 m)
- Strong force dominates
- Quantum field theory required
- Gravity still negligible

### Planck Scale (10^-35 m)
- Quantum gravity essential
- Spacetime structure uncertain
- All forces equally important
- Current theories break down

## Summary: The Scale of Planck Units

**Smallest Observable** → **Planck Scale** → **Largest Observable**

- **Length**: 10^-19 m → 10^-35 m → 10^26 m (spanning 61 orders of magnitude!)
- **Time**: 10^-25 s → 10^-44 s → 10^17 s (spanning 61 orders of magnitude!)
- **Mass**: 10^-31 kg (electron) → 10^-8 kg (Planck) → 10^30 kg (Sun)
- **Energy**: 10^-19 J (photon) → 10^9 J (Planck) → 10^44 J (supernova)
- **Temperature**: 3 K (CMB) → 10^32 K (Planck) → (no upper bound in current universe)

## Visualization Tips

When trying to visualize Planck scales:

1. **Don't try to imagine the actual size** - it's beyond human comprehension
2. **Use ratio thinking** - compare ratios between familiar scales
3. **Use powers of 10** - think in orders of magnitude
4. **Use analogies** - "as small compared to X as X is to Y"
5. **Accept the limitation** - even physicists can't truly "visualize" Planck scale
