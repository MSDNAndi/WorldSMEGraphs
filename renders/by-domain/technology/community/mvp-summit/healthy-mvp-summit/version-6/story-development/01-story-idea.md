## Story Concept

What if navigating the MVP Summit was like playing a roguelike RPG? In this quirky, nerdy take on conference health, Andreas plays the role of a high-level mentor who has beaten this "dungeon" many times, while Marco is a player on his first serious run—accumulating debuffs, managing cooldowns, and learning that the real boss fight isn't the keynote, it's surviving the full conference with HP intact.

Status effects like DEHYDRATED, JET-LAGGED, and DECISION PARALYSIS appear as floating debuffs. Health bars deplete. Energy meters drain. And every health tip is presented as a skill unlock or buff acquisition. It's nerdy, unexpected, and genuinely educational.

## Story Arc Overview

### Act 1: Character Creation Gone Wrong (Panels 1-12)
**Theme: Status effects, starting debuffs, understanding the game**

Marco arrives at the Summit already carrying multiple debuffs: JET-LAGGED, DEHYDRATED, OVER-CAFFEINATED. His HP bar is at 40%. Andreas finds him and begins explaining the game mechanics he's been ignoring.

**Key Educational Moments:**
- **Jet Lag (neuro-052):** "That's the TIME DESYNC debuff. Your internal clock is three zones off. It causes confusion, reduced reaction time, and stacks with sleep deprivation."
- **Dehydration (neuro-051):** "DEHYDRATED gives you -30% Intelligence and -20% Constitution. Every coffee without water makes it worse."
- **Caffeine (pharm-002):** "Coffee is OVERCLOCK—temporary INT buff, but when it wears off, you crash hard. And the half-life is 5-6 hours, so it's still in your system blocking your rest abilities."

**Gaming Elements:**
- Status effect icons floating over Marco's head
- HP/MP bars in 1950s comic style
- "You are carrying too many debuffs" warning

---

### Act 2: The Morning Dungeon (Panels 13-24)
**Theme: Decision paralysis, FOMO, the Keynote Boss**

They enter the conference center—the "Morning Dungeon." Marco immediately faces CHOICE OVERLOAD from the session board. During the keynote, he experiences SCREEN STRAIN. Andreas teaches him to use defensive abilities and avoid trap debuffs.

**Key Educational Moments:**
- **Decision Fatigue (psych-001):** "CHOICE OVERLOAD paralyzes your action queue. Pre-selecting sessions is like setting macros—automate the easy decisions to save willpower for hard ones."
- **FOMO (psych-003):** "FOMO is a trap debuff. It makes you think other players are leveling faster than you. Everyone has it. It's part of the game's design."
- **Eye Strain (eye-051):** "SCREEN STRAIN accumulates every 20 minutes. The 20-20-20 skill clears it: look 20 feet away for 20 seconds. It's on a 20-minute cooldown."

**Gaming Elements:**
- Session board as a "dungeon map"
- Keynote as a "boss encounter"
- Skills unlocking in RPG-style UI boxes

---

### Act 3: The Afternoon Grind (Panels 25-36)
**Theme: Resource management, stamina recovery, party dynamics**

After hours of sessions, Marco's resources are depleted. He's suffering STAGNATION from sitting, his SOCIAL STAMINA is low, and he needs rest. Andreas teaches him about cooldown management and the power of tactical rest.

**Key Educational Moments:**
- **Sedentary Behavior (ph-001):** "STAGNATION debuff. You've been stationary too long—blood pooling, metabolism dropping. Movement is the cleanse. Every 30 minutes, move or take increasing damage."
- **Social Energy (psych-002):** "SOCIAL STAMINA is class-based. Introverts have a smaller pool that drains faster but regenerates in solitude. Extroverts are the opposite. Know your class."
- **Power Naps (neuro-053):** "TACTICAL REST is an OP skill most players ignore. 20 minutes restores 34% of your stats. But if you rest too long, you trigger a sleep cycle and wake up with a daze debuff."

**Gaming Elements:**
- Stamina bar mechanics
- Class descriptions (Introvert/Extrovert builds)
- Rest as an ability with optimal timing

---

### Act 4: Victory Conditions (Panels 37-48)
**Theme: Avoiding traps, optimal strategy, winning the run**

The evening brings the "Networking Tavern"—a social hub with hidden traps. Marco is tempted by the POISON DRINK (alcohol), but Andreas explains why it's a trap that destroys tomorrow's run. They choose the WISDOM TAVERN instead (quieter bar, sparkling water), and Marco finally understands the victory conditions.

**Key Educational Moments:**
- **Alcohol Effects (pharm-001):** "The Networking Tavern has a trap: POISON DRINK. It seems like a buff (+3 Charisma temporarily) but it drains your HP overnight and destroys your rest cycle. Tomorrow's run becomes nearly unwinnable."
- **True Victory:** "The real win condition isn't one perfect day—it's sustainable performance across the whole conference. HP management over DPS maximization."
- **Friendship Theme:** "The best loot isn't items. It's the party members you find who watch your back."

**The Ending:**
They toast with sparkling water. Marco checks his "stats"—all status effects cleared, resources regenerating. Ready for Day Two. The victory condition isn't surviving—it's thriving.
