# Healthy MVP Summit V2 - Story Idea

## Title: "Dawn to Dusk: One Perfect Summit Day"

## Version: 2.0
## Style: 1950s American Comic Book

---

## Educational Objectives

### Focus Areas (Different from V1)
1. **Pre-Summit Preparation** - What to do before you even leave home
2. **Travel Wellness** - Flight tips, layover strategies
3. **Mindfulness** - Mental preparation and presence
4. **Ergonomic Awareness** - Detailed posture and movement tips
5. **Food Timing** - When to eat, not just what
6. **Social Boundaries** - Saying no gracefully
7. **Recovery Techniques** - Active vs passive rest
8. **Energy Management** - Personal energy patterns
9. **Tech Detox** - Balancing screen time
10. **Community Care** - Looking out for others

---

## Character Specifications

### Andreas Erben (Protagonist 1)
- Same visual description as V1
- **Narrative role**: The planner who over-prepared
- **Arc**: Realizes that flexibility matters as much as planning

### Marco Casalaina (Protagonist 2)  
- Same visual description as V1
- **Narrative role**: The improviser who balances structure
- **Arc**: Learns to appreciate systematic approaches

---

## Story Hook

Following Andreas and Marco through a single, complete day at MVP Summit - from pre-dawn preparation to late-night reflection. Every hour brings new challenges and opportunities for healthy choices.

---

## Central Theme

"A healthy day at a conference isn't about perfection - it's about making the best choice available in each moment."

---

## Story Arc (HIGH LEVEL ONLY - NO PANELS)

### Act 1: Before the Sun (5 AM - 7 AM)
- Andreas's morning routine in hotel room
- Yoga/stretching sequence
- Meeting Marco in the lobby
- Pre-breakfast walk around the block
- Mindful breakfast with intention setting

### Act 2: The Morning Sessions (8 AM - 12 PM)
- First session challenges
- Coffee vs. water decisions
- Networking hallway conversations
- Unexpected schedule changes
- Mid-morning movement break

### Act 3: The Midday Challenge (12 PM - 3 PM)
- Lunch crowd navigation
- Food timing discussion
- Dealing with invitation overload
- The critical afternoon period
- Strategic retreat and recovery

### Act 4: Evening Transitions (5 PM - 10 PM)
- End of formal sessions
- The social event dilemma
- Choosing activities wisely
- Looking out for a struggling attendee
- Evening wind-down routine
- Gratitude reflection

---

## Style Notes

### Visual Emphasis
- More close-ups on faces and emotions
- Time stamps visible in panels (5:00 AM, 7:30 AM, etc.)
- Split panels showing contrasting choices
- Dream sequence style for mindfulness moments

### Tone
- More introspective than V1
- Focus on internal dialogue as well as conversation
- Emphasis on the mental health aspect
- Warmth and care for community

---

## Estimated Scope
- Target: 45-48 panels
- Heavy on the morning and afternoon acts
- Mix of action and reflection panels
