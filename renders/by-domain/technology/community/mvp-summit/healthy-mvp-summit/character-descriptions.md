## 1950s Comic Book Style Guidelines

### Visual Style Specifications
- **Art Style**: 1950s American comic book style (like early Marvel/DC)
- **Lines**: Bold black outlines, clean linework
- **Colors**: Flat colors with limited palette, Ben-Day dots for shading
- **Speech Bubbles**: Classic rounded speech bubbles with tails pointing to speakers
- **Thought Bubbles**: Cloud-shaped for internal thoughts
- **Caption Boxes**: Rectangular boxes with brief narrative text
- **Panel Borders**: Bold black borders around each panel

### Color Palette
- **Primary Blue**: #0078D4 (Microsoft Azure blue)
- **Secondary Blue**: #003087 (Darker accent)
- **Green**: #107C10 (Microsoft green for healthy elements)
- **Warm Tones**: #FFB900 (Yellow for energy), #FF8C00 (Orange for warmth)
- **Neutral**: #5C5C5C (Grey for text boxes), #FFFFFF (White backgrounds)
- **Skin Tones**: Appropriate flesh tones with 1950s comic shading

### Typography Notes
- Bold, clean sans-serif fonts in speech bubbles
- Exclamations may have larger, bolder text
- Sound effects in dynamic stylized lettering
