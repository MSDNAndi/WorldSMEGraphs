## Audience Levels

These renderings target adults with limited reading comprehension and little formal education:

**Reading Level**: Grade 4-6 (ages 9-11 equivalent)

**Characteristics**:
- Very short sentences (8-15 words maximum)
- Common everyday vocabulary only
- No technical jargon or complex mathematics
- Concrete analogies to familiar objects
- Step-by-step explanations
- Encouraging, non-intimidating tone
- Visual comparisons (hair, grains of sand, etc.)

## Content Coverage

All renderings cover these topics in simplified form:

1. **Introduction** - What are Planck units?
2. **Planck Length** - The smallest meaningful distance
3. **Planck Time** - The shortest meaningful time
4. **Planck Mass** - The special mass where quantum meets gravity
5. **Planck Energy** - The highest energy concentration
6. **Planck Temperature** - The hottest possible temperature
7. **Why It Matters** - Real-world applications (black holes, Big Bang)
8. **What Happens at Planck Scales** - Quantum foam, uncertainty
9. **Why We Can't Measure Them** - The measurement paradox
10. **Current Research** - String theory, loop quantum gravity
11. **Summary** - Key takeaways

## Usage Guidelines

**For Educators**:
- Use these renderings as introductory material before technical content
- Pair with visual aids and demonstrations
- Allow 10-15 minutes for reading and discussion
- Follow up with questions to assess comprehension

**For Self-Learners**:
- Start with your native language version
- Read at your own pace
- Don't worry about understanding everything perfectly
- Focus on the big ideas first
- Re-read sections as needed

**For Translators**:
- Maintain simple sentence structure
- Use everyday vocabulary in target language
- Adapt cultural examples appropriately
- Keep the encouraging, accessible tone
- Validate readability with native speakers

## Quality Standards

All renderings must meet these criteria:

1. **Readability**: Grade 4-6 level (Flesch-Kincaid)
2. **Sentence Length**: Average ≤15 words, max 20 words
3. **Vocabulary**: Common words only (no technical jargon)
4. **Structure**: Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
5. **Examples**: Concrete, relatable analogies
6. **Tone**: Encouraging, non-intimidating
7. **Accuracy**: Faithful to source material (no scientific errors)
8. **Length**: 800-1200 words (5-7 minute read)
9. **Formatting**: Clear headings, good visual hierarchy
10. **Accessibility**: Screen-reader friendly, clear structure
