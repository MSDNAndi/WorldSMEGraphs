# Number Theory

## Overview
This domain encompasses the mathematical study of integers and integer-valued functions, with a focus on the properties of natural numbers, prime numbers, and special number forms. Number theory is one of the oldest branches of mathematics, with roots in ancient civilizations.

## Domain Path
`science/math/number-theory`

## Subdomains

### 1. Prime Numbers
**Path**: `primes/`  
**Status**: 10 AKUs created  
**Focus**: Properties, distribution, and applications of prime numbers

Prime numbers are the fundamental multiplicative building blocks of integers. This subdomain covers:
- Basic definition and properties
- Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic
- Prime generation algorithms (Sieve of Eratosthenes)
- Cross-domain applications (cryptography, biology, physics, computer science, economics)
- Distribution theorems and conjectures

### 2. Composite Numbers
**Path**: `composite-numbers/`  
**Status**: 1 AKU created  
**Focus**: Numbers with multiple divisors, complement to primes

Composite numbers complete the partition of natural numbers > 1. This subdomain covers:
- Definition and basic properties
- Factorization
- Relationship to primes
- Applications in cryptography (factoring problem)

### 3. Perfect Numbers
**Path**: `perfect-numbers/`  
**Status**: 1 AKU created  
**Focus**: Numbers equal to sum of their proper divisors

Perfect numbers are among the most studied special numbers in mathematics. This subdomain covers:
- Definition and examples
- Euclid-Euler theorem connecting to Mersenne primes
- Only 51 known perfect numbers
- Unsolved problem: Do odd perfect numbers exist?

### 4. Mersenne Primes
**Path**: `mersenne-primes/`  
**Status**: 1 AKU created  
**Focus**: Primes of form 2^p - 1

Mersenne primes are the largest known primes due to efficient testing. This subdomain covers:
- Definition and properties
- Lucas-Lehmer test
- GIMPS (distributed computing project)
- Fundamental connection to perfect numbers
- Only 51 known Mersenne primes

### 5. Fermat Primes
**Path**: `fermat-primes/`  
**Status**: 1 AKU created  
**Focus**: Extremely rare primes of form 2^(2^n) + 1

Only 5 Fermat primes are known, with remarkable geometric connections. This subdomain covers:
- Definition and the 5 known Fermat primes
- Fermat's incorrect conjecture
- Gauss's construction theorem (constructible polygons)
- Connection to geometry
- Open problems

### 6. Fibonacci Sequence
**Path**: `fibonacci/`  
**Status**: 1 AKU created  
**Focus**: Famous recursive sequence with widespread applications

The Fibonacci sequence appears throughout mathematics and nature. This subdomain covers:
- Recursive definition
- Golden ratio connection (Binet's formula)
- Fibonacci primes
- Applications in nature (phyllotaxis)
- Computer science applications (recursion examples)

### 7. Amicable Numbers
**Path**: `amicable-numbers/`  
**Status**: 1 AKU created  
**Focus**: Pairs where each equals sum of other's proper divisors

Amicable numbers have fascinated mathematicians for over 2500 years. This subdomain covers:
- Definition and smallest pair (220, 284)
- Thābit ibn Qurra's rule
- Over 12 million known pairs
- Historical and cultural significance
- Open problems (odd amicable pairs?)

## Key Relationships

### Within Number Theory
- **Primes ↔ Composites**: Complementary partition of ℕ > 1
- **Primes → Perfect Numbers**: Via Mersenne primes and Euclid-Euler theorem
- **Mersenne Primes ↔ Perfect Numbers**: 1-1 correspondence for even perfects
- **Primes → Fibonacci**: Fibonacci primes are intersection
- **Perfect Numbers → Amicable Numbers**: Generalization from self to mutual reference

### Cross-Domain Connections
- **→ Cryptography**: RSA encryption (primes, factoring problem)
- **→ Biology**: Cicada life cycles (primes), plant patterns (Fibonacci)
- **→ Physics**: Quantum chaos (prime gaps)
- **→ Computer Science**: Hash tables (primes), algorithms (Fibonacci)
- **→ Geometry**: Constructible polygons (Fermat primes)
- **→ Economics**: Financial security (primes in crypto)

## Historical Significance
Number theory contains some of humanity's oldest mathematical knowledge:
- **Ancient (>2000 BCE)**: Recognition of primes
- **~300 BCE**: Euclid's Elements (primes, perfect numbers)
- **~500 BCE**: Pythagoreans (amicable numbers)
- **1202 CE**: Fibonacci introduces his sequence to Europe
- **1640**: Fermat's (incorrect) conjecture about Fermat primes
- **1732**: Euler disproves Fermat, factors F₅
- **1796**: 19-year-old Gauss proves 17-gon constructible
- **1996-present**: GIMPS finds most recent Mersenne primes
